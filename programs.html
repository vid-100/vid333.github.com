Army

Army training can include basic military training, leadership training, and advanced military training: Basic military training This training includes physical training, drill, weapon training, fieldcraft, and tactics. The goal is to develop discipline, physical fitness, and basic military skills. Leadership training This training includes leadership camps, outdoor exercises, and war games. The goal is to develop leadership qualities and decision-making abilities. Advanced military training This training includes specialized training and combat training. The goal is to prepare officers for specific roles within the army.

Navy

Initial training: Cadets undergo initial training at the Indian Naval Academy (INA) for four years and 22 weeks, respectively. Officer training: Training for officers in the Indian Navy varies depending on the entry scheme: National Defence Academy (NDA) Entry: Four years of training, including three years at NDA, Pune, and one year at INA, Ezhimala 10+2 B.Tech Cadet Entry Scheme: Four years of training, including one year at INA, Ezhimala, and three years at a technical institution Direct Entry Graduate Cadets: Nine months to one year at INA, Ezhimala Short Service Commission (SSC) Officers: Nine months at INA, Ezhimala .

Air Force

The Indian Air Force (IAF) offers a variety of military training programs, including: Air Force Academy (AFA): Stage-I training: Six months of joint training for trainees in Flying, Technical, and Non-Technical ground duty branches Stage-II training: 24 weeks of training for pilot trainees in their respective Flying Training Establishments Stage-III training: For successful pilot trainees who are commissioned as Officers Training for Non-Technical Ground Duty branches: Trainees continue their Stage-II training at the Academy for another 24 weeks Training for Administration (Air Traffic Control): Trainees continue at AFA for another 24 weeks of specialized training Training for Administration (Fighter Controller): Trainees proceed to Air Defence College at Memaura for 24 weeks of specialized training Training for Meteorology branch: Trainees proceed to Air Force Administrative College for 16 weeks of training .

BSF

Basic training for a BSF jawan is 52 weeks, followed by several years of on-the-job training. BSF training includes physical fitness, tactical drills, simulations, and classroom instruction. Physical fitness is a key focus, with recruits undergoing intensive physical exercises and endurance training. Classroom instruction covers topics such as law enforcement, human rights, border management, and first aid.

CRPF

    The Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF) is a paramilitary force of the Union of India that performs a range of law enforcement and internal security duties: Established in 1939 as the Crown Representative's Police, the CRPF was renamed in 1949. Role The CRPF's duties include:

  • Maintaining law and order
  • Counter-insurgency operations
  • Anti-Naxal operations
  • Crowd control
  • Riot control
  • VIP protection
  • Environmental conservation
  • Rescue and relief operations

ITBP

The Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP) Force was raised on 24 October, 1962. At present, the ITBP guards 3,488 km long India-China borders ranging from the Karakoram Pass in Ladakh to Jachep La in Arunachal Pradesh. The Indo Tibetan Border Police (ITBP) offers a variety of training programs to prepare its personnel for the demanding tasks of guarding the Himalayan borders: Basic Training A 44-week program that includes physical training, unarmed combat, drill, weapon training, map reading, and more. The goal is to develop soldiers with the required physical fitness, soldierly skills, and team spirit.

RAILWAY

Training for RPF personnel The RPF Zonal Training Centre in Tiruchchirappalli offers training for constable recruits, including an 8-month initial training program, an 8-week orientation course, and a 4-week refresher course. The center also offers capsule courses on a variety of topics, including soft skills, ethics, behavioral skills, and disaster management. Training for RPF officers The RPF Training Centre in Subedarganj, Prayagraj, Uttar Pradesh has a training capacity of 100 trainees. Training for cadets at the National Defence Academy The Army Training Team at the National Defence Academy trains cadets in basic soldiering skills, including weapon training, map reading, radio telephony, and field engineering.

POLICE

The Indian Police Service (abbr. IPS) is a civil service under the All India Services. It replaced the Indian Imperial Police in 1948, a year after India became independent from the British Empire. The training for Indian Police Service (IPS) officers is a two-year program that includes four phases: Foundation Course: This three-month course is held at the LBSNAA in Mussoorie and is shared by all new UPSC CSE recruits. Basic Course: This 11-month phase is held at the Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel National Police Academy (SVPNPA) in Hyderabad. The course's goal is to prepare probationers for the challenges they'll face in the field. District Practical Training: This six-month phase takes place at the respective cadre. Phase II: This one-month phase is held at the SVPNPA in Hyderabad. During their training, IPS officers receive a salary of Rs 15,600–Rs 39,100
